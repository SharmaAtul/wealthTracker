Cash flow Planning

Definition:

A plan that shows the sources, amounts, and timing of income and expenses over a time period in the future. The results of cash flow planning is a budget and the depending on the amount of income or expenses at the end of each year you have a cash-flow surplus or deficit.

Target:

Anyone that wants to know whether planned expenses can be met from cash surplus or if some form of credit is required.
Reason:

To ensure that clients live within their means and reduce the potential of expensive non-deductable consumer debt.  Used by financial planners to project clients wealth creation and retirement savings forwards.
